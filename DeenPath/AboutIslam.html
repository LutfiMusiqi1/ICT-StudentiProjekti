About Islam

Islam is a monotheistic Abrahamic religion founded on submission to the will of Allah (God). It is the second-largest religion in the world, with over a billion followers. The word "Islam" itself means "submission" and is derived from the same root as "peace" in Arabic, reflecting the faith's emphasis on finding peace through submission to God.

Core Beliefs of Islam

  • Tawheed (Oneness of God): The belief in the absolute oneness and uniqueness of Allah.
  • Prophets: Belief in all prophets sent by God, with Muhammad ﷺ as the final messenger.
  • Books: Belief in the revealed scriptures, especially the Qur’an as the final and unchanged word of God.
  • Angels: Belief in the existence of angels created by Allah.
  • Day of Judgment: Belief in the resurrection and the final judgment.
  • Divine Decree (Qadar): Belief in Allah’s complete knowledge and control over destiny.

The Five Pillars of Islam

  • Shahada (Faith): The declaration that there is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His Messenger. This testimony is the entry point into Islam.
  • Salah (Prayer): Muslims perform five daily prayers at prescribed times, serving as a direct link between the worshipper and Allah.
  • Zakat (Charity): Muslims are required to give a portion of their wealth to those in need, purifying their wealth and supporting the community.
  • Sawm (Fasting): During the month of Ramadan, Muslims fast from dawn to sunset, fostering self-discipline and empathy for the less fortunate.
  • Hajj (Pilgrimage): Muslims who are able must perform the pilgrimage to Mecca at least once in their lifetime, symbolizing unity and equality.

Brief History of Islam

History: Islam began in the 7th century CE in the cities of Mecca and Medina in Arabia. Prophet Muhammad ﷺ received the first revelation at the age of 40. Facing early opposition in Mecca, he and his followers migrated to Medina (the Hijrah), marking the start of the Islamic calendar. After the Prophet’s passing, Islam spread rapidly across Asia, Africa, and Europe during the Caliphate era.

The Qur'an

The Qur’an is the central religious text of Islam, revealed to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ over 23 years. Written in Arabic, it has remained unchanged since its revelation and is regarded by Muslims as the literal word of God, providing guidance for all aspects of life.

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was born in 570 CE in Mecca and was known as Al-Ameen (the trustworthy). He is considered the final messenger in a long line of prophets. His life and teachings (the Sunnah) serve as a model for Muslims worldwide.

Islamic Ethics & Way of Life

Ethics: Islam emphasizes kindness, honesty, justice, and compassion. Family values, respect for parents, neighbors, and animals are central. Daily conduct is guided by the Qur’an and the Sunnah of the Prophet.

Common Misconceptions

  • Islam and Peace: Islam promotes peace and forbids injustice and oppression.
  • Role of Women: Islam grants women rights and dignity; many misconceptions arise from cultural practices, not the faith itself.
  • Media Myths: Many negative stereotypes about Islam are not based on its true teachings.
