Prayer (Salah)

Learn how to perform daily prayers and their significance in a Muslim’s life.

Importance of Prayer

Salah (prayer) is one of the Five Pillars of Islam and the most important act of worship after the declaration of faith. It is a direct link between the worshipper and Allah, bringing spiritual peace, discipline, and a sense of community. Through prayer, Muslims remember Allah, seek guidance, and renew their faith throughout the day.

The Five Daily Prayers

Fajr

Time: Dawn

Fard: 2 Rak‘ahs

Sunnah: 2 Rak‘ahs before Fard (highly recommended)

Virtue: The Prophet ﷺ said: "The two Rak‘ahs of Fajr are better than the world and all it contains."

Dhuhr

Time: Noon

Fard: 4 Rak‘ahs

Sunnah: 4 before + 2 after Fard (recommended)

Virtue: Midday prayer brings peace and a break from daily work.

Asr

Time: Afternoon

Fard: 4 Rak‘ahs

Sunnah: 4 before Fard (optional)

Virtue: The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever prays before Asr four Rak‘ahs, Allah will forbid his body from the Fire."

Maghrib

Time: Sunset

Fard: 3 Rak‘ahs

Sunnah: 2 after Fard (recommended)

Virtue: The first prayer after sunset, marking the end of the day.

Isha

Time: Night

Fard: 4 Rak‘ahs

Sunnah: 2 after Fard (recommended), plus Witr prayer (highly recommended)

Virtue: The Prophet ﷺ encouraged delaying Isha (not too late) and praying Witr before sleep.

How to Perform Salah: Step-by-Step

  1. Face the Qiblah: Stand facing the direction of the Ka‘bah in Mecca.
  2. Intention (Niyyah): Make a sincere intention in your heart for the specific prayer.
  3. Takbir: Raise your hands and say “Allahu Akbar” to begin the prayer.
  4. Recitation: Recite Surah Al-Fatiha and another surah or verses from the Qur’an.
  5. Rukūʿ (Bowing): Bow and say “Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem.”
  6. Sujūd (Prostration): Prostrate and say “Subhana Rabbiyal A‘la.”
  7. Sitting (Jalsa): Sit briefly between the two prostrations.
  8. Repeat: Repeat the recitations, bowing, and prostration according to the number of units (rak‘ahs).
  9. Tashahhud & Salams: Sit and recite the final testimony, then end the prayer by turning your head right and left saying “Assalamu Alaikum wa Rahmatullah.”

Tips for a Better Prayer

  • Face the Qiblah: Always ensure you are facing Mecca.
  • Focus (Khushu‘): Try to concentrate and avoid distractions.
  • Cleanliness: Make sure your body, clothes, and place of prayer are clean.
  • Pray on Time: Perform each prayer within its prescribed time.
