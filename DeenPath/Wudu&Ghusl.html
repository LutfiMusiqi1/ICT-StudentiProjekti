How to Perform Wudu

Wudu is the ritual washing Muslims perform before prayer. It is a requirement for the validity of Salah.

  1. Make intention (Niyyah): Have a sincere intention in your heart to purify for prayer.
  2. Wash hands three times: Wash both hands up to the wrists three times, starting with the right.
  3. Rinse mouth and nose: Rinse your mouth and nostrils three times each.
  4. Wash face: Wash your entire face from forehead to chin and ear to ear three times.
  5. Wash arms up to elbows: Wash both arms, right then left, from fingertips to elbows three times.
  6. Wipe head and ears: Wet your hands and wipe over your head once, then wipe inside and behind your ears.
  7. Wash feet: Wash both feet up to the ankles three times, starting with the right.

How to Perform Ghusl

Ghusl is the full-body ritual purification required after major impurity, such as after marital relations or menstruation.

  1. Make intention (Niyyah): Intend in your heart to purify yourself for worship.
  2. Wash private parts: Clean any impurity from the private parts thoroughly.
  3. Perform full Wudu: Complete a full Wudu as described above.
  4. Pour water over head and body: Pour water over your head three times, then wash your entire body, ensuring water reaches every part.
